PROJECT: AURELIAN STUDIO - PREMIUM FURNITURE CATALOG
================================================================================

1. PROJECT IDENTITY & IDEA
--------------------------
Aurelian Studio is a high-end, immersive digital brochure/catalog designed to 
elevate furniture browsing into a cinematic experience. Unlike traditional 
e-commerce sites, it focuses on the "story of craft"—moving from initial 
sketch to raw materials, joinery, and finally the finished product in a 
lifestyle setting.

Core Tech Stack:
- Framework: Next.js 16 (App Router)
- Styling: Tailwind CSS v4
- Animation: Framer Motion
- Components: Lucide React (Icons)
- Deployment: Optimized for Vercel

3. CORE WORKFLOWS
-----------------
A. The Cinematic Entry:
   - User lands on / (page.tsx).
   - Experience: An 8-second slow transition from Day (hero-day.jpeg) to 
     Night (hero-night.jpeg) occurs, symbolizing the timeless nature of the pieces.
   - Action: User enters the "Archive" (Catalog).

B. The Discovery Phase:
   - User navigates through spreads in Catalog.tsx.
   - Experience: Horizontal/Spread-based layout mimicking a physical luxury magazine.
   - Interactive Hotspots: Clicking hotspots on room images reveals product cards.

C. Story Mode (Genesis):
   - Clicking a product enters its specific Story Mode (/catalog/[id]).
   - Workflow: The site plays a scroll-driven or timed sequence defined in 
     the `introSequence` of the data.
     (Canvas/Room -> Blueprint/Sketch -> Skeleton/Build -> Vision/Compose -> Masterpiece/Reveal).

D. Product Deep-Dive & Lead Gen:
   - After the story, the user reaches the product detail page.
   - Experience: High-res galleries, technical specs (Weight, Lead Time, 
     Craft Notes), and "Designed For" context.
   - Action: Clicking "Enquire" opens the EnquireModal to capture user interest.

4. TECHNICAL HIGHLIGHTS
-----------------------
- Tailwind CSS v4: Uses modern CSS-first configuration in globals.css.
- Framer Motion: Used for complex page transitions and "reveal" effects 
  in the Story Mode.
- Data Modeling: Highly structured products with metadata for craft, 
  materials, and construction phases.

5. CONTENT MANAGEMENT
---------------------
To add or update products, edit `src/data/catalog.ts`. The structure allows 
for easy extension of the "introSequence" to add more storytelling layers 
to any piece of furniture.
